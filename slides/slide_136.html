01

The Code of Color

Color has evolved from a strict code of hierarchy to a tool for expression. From the divine Gold of Egypt and the status-signaling Imperial Purple of Rome to the psychological pastels of Rococo, pigment has always signaled power, sanctity, or rebellion.

02

Material Integrity vs. Illusion

Design history oscillates between concealing structure and revealing it. The Arts & Crafts movement's demand for "truth to materials" (exposed wood, honest linen) stands in direct ethical contrast to the theatrical illusions of Baroque stucco or faux finishes.

03

Synthetic Modernity

The 20th century marked a pivotal shift where "new" became more valuable than "precious." The Art Deco embrace of Bakelite, chrome, and rayon signaled that scientific novelty and machine-age aesthetics had replaced natural rarity as the ultimate signifier of modern identity.
