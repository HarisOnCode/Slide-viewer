04. Roman Era Context

Men's Garments:
Power & Status

The Toga Virilis

More than clothing, the toga was a civic uniform restricted to Roman citizens. The heavy woolen drape required assistance to arrange, signaling wealth (slaves) and leisure, while specific borders (purple stripe) denoted senatorial rank.

Military Dress

Practicality reigned on campaign: short tunics, heavy cloaks (sagum), and trousers (braccae) adopted from conquered Gauls for warmth.

Caligae

The heavy-soled, hobnailed military sandals that allowed legions to march across the empire—functional design enabling conquest.
