The Sheath & The Kilt

The foundation of Egyptian dress was the simple tunic and kilt. For women, the kalasiris or sheath dress created a columnar silhouette that mirrored architectural forms. Men wore the shendyt, a kilt wrapped around the waist, varying in length and complexity based on status.

Textile Mastery: Pleating

Linen was the primary fabric, chosen for purity and climate suitability. Egyptian weavers achieved remarkable fineness, creating "royal linen" that was almost transparent. They developed permanent pleating techniques, adding texture and volume to simple rectangular construction.

"Silhouette was governed by social hierarchy—the higher the status, the finer the linen and the more elaborate the draping."
