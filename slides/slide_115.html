Paris Métro Entrance (c. 1900)
Hector Guimard's iconic cast-iron structures transformed public infrastructure into organic sculpture, bringing the avant-garde to the street.
12. Art Nouveau Context

Architecture as
Living Organism

The Total Work of Art

Victor Horta and Hector Guimard redefined space through the Gesamtkunstwerk concept. Structure was not hidden but celebrated; iron columns bloomed into "whiplash" curves, and stained glass bathed interiors in botanical light, seamlessly merging function with ornament.

Metropolitan Identity

Guimard's Paris Métro entrances utilized industrial materials—modular cast iron and glass—to create fluid, vegetal forms. This marked a radical shift where modern industrial methods were used to create uniquely organic, non-historical urban landmarks.

"I want to make the structure sing. Iron, glass, and stone must flow together like the stems of a vine."
