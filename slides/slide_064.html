From Tunic to Tailoring

Men's fashion evolved from loose tunics to fitted garments like the cotehardie and doublet. The "gambeson"—a padded defensive jacket—influenced civilian wear, creating a structured, athletic silhouette.

Key Garments & Details

"Chausses" (separate hose legs) were tied to the doublet with "points" (laces). Accessories like belts ("girdles") carried purses and daggers, while headwear ranged from simple coifs to elaborate chaperons.

"The shift to fitted garments required advanced tailoring skills—buttons and lacing replaced simple drape."
