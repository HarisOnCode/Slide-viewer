87 Rococo / Grooming
The Artifice of Beauty

Powder & Patch

Rococo beauty celebrated artificiality over nature. Faces were painted porcelain-white with lead-based powders, accented by circular spots of bright rouge. "Mouches" (beauty patches) made of silk or velvet were strategically placed to hide blemishes or signal flirtatious intent, while hair towered in powdered, decorated poufs.
