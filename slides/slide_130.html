Greek Era

Ideal Harmony

Ethos

Focused on the search for perfection, mathematical proportion (Golden Ratio), and idealized human forms. Design was philosophical, democratic, and restrained. Garments like the chiton emphasized natural drape and the body in motion.

Roman Era

Power & Realism

Imperium

Focused on engineering, spectacle, and projecting state power. Art leaned toward veristic (realistic) portraiture and grand narratives. Design was persuasive and hierarchical, with strict sartorial codes (togas) defining status and citizenship.
