The Rococo Style

Departing from Baroque grandeur, Rococo art emphasized intimacy, asymmetry, and lightheartedness. Paintings moved from religious awe to secular pleasure, featuring soft brushwork, pastel palettes, and themes of love and nature.

François Boucher

Defined the era with mythological scenes, voluptuous forms, and soft, pearly skin tones.

Jean-Honoré Fragonard

Master of swift brushwork and playful, erotic themes capturing the frivolous spirit of the age.

Jean-Marc Nattier

Fashionable portraitist known for depicting court ladies as allegorical figures in soft light.

Decorative Arts & Porcelain

Sèvres porcelain became the ultimate status symbol, integrating painting, sculpture, and gilding. These objects mirrored fashion textiles with their meticulous floral patterns and signature colors like "Rose Pompadour."
