07. Rococo Era

Asymmetry &
Playful Ornament

Rocailles & S-Curves

The visual language shifted from Baroque grandeur to intimate caprice. The signature rocaille (shell-work) motif introduced fluid, asymmetrical forms resembling rocks, shells, and C-scrolls that defied classical orders.

Textile Lightness

Heavy velvets were replaced by airy silks, taffetas, and intricate lace. Floral patterns became smaller and more naturalistic, often meandering in serpentine lines that echoed the era's furniture legs and panel moldings.

"Rococo design rejected straight lines for the curve, favoring lightness, asymmetry, and a playful sense of movement over rigid symmetry."
