- DESIGN HISTORY CONTEXT

Rococo

1715 – 1730

The Régence Transition

Aristocracy leaves Versailles for Paris. Rise of the hôtel particulier (townhouse) and intimate salon gatherings.

1730 – 1750

High Rococo & Asymmetry

The peak of rocaille (shell) ornamentation. Meissonnier defines the style with S-curves and elaborate whimsy.

1750 – 1764

The Pompadour Era

Madame de Pompadour patronizes the arts. Soft pastels, Sèvres porcelain, and intellectual salon culture flourish.

1770 – 1780

Pastoral Fantasy & Decline

Marie Antoinette's Petit Trianon aesthetic. A shift toward "rustic" simplicity before Neoclassicism takes over.
