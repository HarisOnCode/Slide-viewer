07. Rococo Era Context

Aristocratic Leisure
& The Salon

The Shift to Intimacy

Moving away from the rigid grandeur of Versailles, aristocratic life shifted to intimate city mansions (hôtels particuliers). Social life centered on the salon—spaces designed for witty conversation, intellectual exchange, and refined pleasure.

Decorative Arts Flourish

Interior design became paramount. Furniture, porcelain (Sèvres), and textiles were unified in a light, airy aesthetic featuring asymmetry, pastel colors, and nature motifs like shells (rocaille) and flowers.

"Design softened into a language of pleasure, prioritizing comfort, privacy, and the delicate art of living beautifully."
