The Toga & Tunica

While the simple tunica was the base layer for all classes, the toga was the distinct mark of the Roman male citizen. This vast semi-circle of wool was draped to deliberately impede active labor, signaling leisure, power, and civil duty.

Sumptuary Laws

Garments were strictly regulated by law to enforce social hierarchy. Only citizens could wear togas; purple stripes (clavi) denoted rank (Senator vs. Equestrian). For women, the stola indicated marriage and virtue, distinguishing them from others.

"The garment was not a personal choice, but a billboard of citizenship, legal status, and moral character."
