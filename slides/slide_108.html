Biological Modernism

Rejecting historical eclecticism, Art Nouveau embraced the asymmetry of the natural world. Architects translated biological forms—stems, tendrils, and insect wings—into structural elements, freezing movement in metal.

Synthesis of Arts

The movement dissolved the hierarchy between fine arts (painting, sculpture) and applied arts (furniture, gates). It championed the Gesamtkunstwerk (total work of art), where mass production met high craft.

"We must have a new art. The art of the future must be a synthesis of all the arts."
