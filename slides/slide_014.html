Mobiliary vs. Parietal Art

Stone Age art divided into two realms: Parietal art (immovable cave murals like Lascaux, often located deep within the earth) and Mobiliary art (portable objects like the Venus figurines, carved bone, and tools). Both forms served as functional magic rather than mere decoration.

The Anonymous Masters

There were no signed names, but mastery was evident. "Artists" were likely shamans or spiritual leaders who understood the materials. The creation process was collective—a shared visual language that bonded the tribe across generations.

"These were the first designers: individuals who understood that an image could hold power, tell a story, and ensure the survival of the group."
