The Three-Piece Suit Origins

The modern suit finds its roots here with the introduction of the justaucorps (long coat), waistcoat, and breeches. This ensemble replaced the doublet and hose, shifting the male silhouette towards a vertical, imposing line.

Wigs & Neckwear

The periwig became an essential status symbol, growing larger and more elaborate. Neckwear evolved from stiff ruffs to the falling lace collar and eventually the cravat, often made of expensive Venetian lace.

Footwear & Stance

Red heels (talons rouges) were strictly reserved for nobility at Versailles. Shoes featured square toes and large decorative buckles, forcing a deliberate, turned-out stance.
