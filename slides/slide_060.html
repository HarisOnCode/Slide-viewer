05. Medieval Era Visuals

Hierarchies of
Meaning

Icon Layers & Gothic Script

Medieval visual language was strictly hierarchical. Layouts prioritized sacred text in blackletter (Textura), surrounded by glosses and illustrations. Scale indicated spiritual importance rather than perspective depth.

Narrative Surfaces

From tapestries to stained glass, surfaces became storytelling mediums for an illiterate populace. Marginalia introduced secular humor and daily life into sacred contexts, creating a complex dual narrative.

"The page was a cathedral in miniature—structured, ornamented, and designed to lead the eye toward the divine."
