The Artistic Dress

Rejecting the rigid corsetry and artificial silhouettes of Victorian fashion, the movement championed "Artistic Dress." Loose-fitting smocks, natural waistlines, and fluid drapery prioritized health, comfort, and natural beauty over restrictive societal norms.

The Holistic Home

William Morris envisioned the home as a total work of art where every object—from wallpaper to wardrobe—harmonized. Textiles, furniture, and garments were designed with the same ethical principles: honest materials, visible construction, and nature-inspired motifs.

"Have nothing in your house that you do not know to be useful, or believe to be beautiful." — William Morris
