Canon & Glyph

The Canon of Proportion

Egyptian art relied on a strict grid system rather than optical perspective. The "Canon of Proportion" ensured consistency for millennia. Figures were idealized in "Frontalism"—depicting the head in profile, shoulders frontally, and legs in profile to capture the body's most complete aspect.

Sacred Carvings

Art and writing were inseparable. Hieroglyphs (sacred carvings) functioned as both decorative borders and essential captions, organizing wall space into registers that narrated rituals, lineage, and daily life with graphic precision.

"To draw a figure was not to capture a fleeting moment, but to create an eternal truth legible to the gods."
