Evolution of the Classical Ideal

"The aim of art is to represent not the outward appearance of things, but their inward significance."

- ARISTOTLE

03

c. 800 – 480 BCE

Archaic Period

The structural dawn. Development of the Doric order, Kouros figures with rigid stances, and the dominance of black-figure pottery. A focus on stylized pattern over realism.

c. 480 – 323 BCE

Classical Golden Age

The height of idealized realism. Contrapposto stance in sculpture (The Spear Bearer), construction of the Parthenon, and the refinement of the Ionic order. Balance, harmony, and reason.

c. 323 – 31 BCE

Hellenistic Period

Design becomes dynamic and emotional. Marked by dramatic movement (Laocoön, Winged Victory), ornate Corinthian columns, and the spread of Greek aesthetics across the Mediterranean.
