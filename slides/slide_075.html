The Rigid Bodice

Baroque fashion introduced structured formality. Women's silhouettes were defined by the rigid corps à baleine (boned bodice or stays) which elongated the torso and flattened the bust, contrasting sharply with full, gathered skirts.

Masculine Evolution

Men's fashion transitioned from the doublet and hose to the early three-piece suit. The justaucorps (knee-length coat), waistcoat, and breeches replaced the shorter doublet, creating a vertical line emphasizing height and dignity.

"Clothing became architecture for the body—constructing an image of absolute power through rigid lines and expansive volume."
