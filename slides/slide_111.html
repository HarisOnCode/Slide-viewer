The Poster as Art

Art Nouveau erased the line between "high art" and commercial design. Color lithography allowed for mass production of large-scale posters, turning Paris boulevards into open-air museums known as the "affichomanie" (poster mania).

Integrated Typography

Text was no longer separate information but an integral part of the design. Letterforms curved and flowed like plant stems, wrapping around figures and frames, creating a unified visual language where image and message became inseparable.

"The street became an art gallery for the people, democratizing beauty through the medium of the poster."
