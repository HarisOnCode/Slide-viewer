The Poster Revolution

Art Deco revolutionized visual communication through bold, reductive graphics. Artists like A.M. Cassandre merged typography with image, using airbrush techniques and dynamic perspectives to celebrate speed, travel, and luxury commodities.

Typography & Retail

Typefaces became geometric and sans-serif (e.g., Broadway, Bifur), reflecting industrial efficiency. Department stores transformed retail into theater, using dramatic lighting, chrome fixtures, and streamlined mannequins to sell a modern lifestyle.

"The street became a gallery. Posters and neon signs created a new urban landscape of desire, selling not just products, but the dream of modernity itself."
