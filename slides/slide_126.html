10. Art Deco (c. 1919–1939)

Geometric Luxury

Platinum, Onyx & The Rise of Bakelite

126

01

Platinum & Diamonds

The "white" look dominated high jewelry. Platinum settings allowed for delicate, lacy geometric designs that held diamonds securely with minimal visible metal.

02

Bakelite Revolution

Early plastics like Bakelite brought vibrant colors and affordable luxury to the masses. Carved bangles and geometric brooches became fashion staples.

03

Black Onyx Contrast

Stark contrasts defined the era. Black onyx paired with diamonds or coral created bold, graphic statements that echoed the sharp lines of modern architecture.

04

Exotic Motifs (Cartier)

Egyptian and "exotic" influences permeated design. Cartier's iconic panther motifs and Tutankhamun-inspired pieces blended luxury materials with ancient symbolism.
