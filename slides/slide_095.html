Arts & Crafts
Timeline

1860 – 1910 CE

"Have nothing in your houses that you do not know to be useful, or believe to be beautiful."

- WILLIAM MORRIS

1851 – 1860

Reaction to Industry

Great Exhibition of 1851 sparks critique of mass production; John Ruskin publishes "The Stones of Venice" advocating for medieval craft values.

1861

Morris, Marshall, Faulkner & Co.

William Morris founds "The Firm," establishing the movement's commercial base for handcrafted textiles, furniture, and stained glass.

1880s – 1890s

Guild Revival & Expansion

Formation of the Art Workers' Guild (1884) and Arts and Crafts Exhibition Society (1888); style spreads to America (Stickley) and Europe.

1900 – 1910

Transition to Modernism

Movement principles influence the Deutsche Werkbund and Bauhaus; shift from decorative craft to functional simplicity.
