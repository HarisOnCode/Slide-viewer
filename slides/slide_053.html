Vitruvius & Architecture

Marcus Vitruvius Pollio defined the "Vitruvian Triad": firmitas, utilitas, venustas (strength, utility, beauty). This philosophy underpinned Roman design, from the monumental Colosseum to aqueducts and domestic villas.

Augustan Classicism

Under Emperor Augustus, art became state propaganda. Idealized naturalism merged with Greek forms to project stability and divine favor. Frescoes evolved through four distinct styles, creating illusionistic depth on flat walls.
