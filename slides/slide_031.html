03. Greek Era Context

Democracy &
Civic Design

The Rise of the Polis

The shift from palaces to the polis (city-state) democratized design. Art moved into public spaces like the agora and theaters, serving not just kings, but the citizenry. Architecture sought ideal proportions to reflect a rational universe.

Theater & Athletics

Civic life revolved around performance and competition. Theaters were engineering marvels of acoustics, while athletics celebrated the human form. This focus on the body directly influenced garment draping and the "Canon" of anatomical perfection.
