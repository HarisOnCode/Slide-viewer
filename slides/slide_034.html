The Language of Orders

Greek visual communication was codified through architectural "Orders"—systems of proportion and detail that communicated gender and character. Doric (masculine/sturdy), Ionic (feminine/scrolls), and Corinthian (ornate/floral) established a visual hierarchy still used in branding today.

Narrative on Ceramics

Unlike static Egyptian registers, Greek vase painting introduced dynamic storytelling. Figures in motion, interacting in realistic space, turned everyday objects into mediums for myth, politics, and social values—the precursor to modern graphic narrative.

"Greek design wasn't just structure; it was a legible language where every column capital and painted gesture told a story of ideal proportion."
