10. Art Deco Context

Modernity,
Speed & Luxury

Machine Aesthetic

Art Deco embraced the machine age, celebrating technology through streamlined forms, geometric repetition, and sleek surfaces. It rejected historical revivalism in favor of a bold, forward-looking visual language symbolizing progress.

Material Opulence

Designers combined industrial materials like chrome, glass, and plastic with traditional luxury elements—ivory, jade, and exotic woods. This synthesis created objects that felt both mass-producable and exceptionally exclusive.

"Streamlining extended beyond vehicles to everyday objects, turning toasters and radios into icons of velocity and efficiency."
