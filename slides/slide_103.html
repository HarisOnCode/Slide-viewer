The Green Dining Room (1866)
A pioneering example of the total interior aesthetic designed by William Morris and Philip Webb for the South Kensington Museum.
08. Arts & Crafts Movement

Art Forms &
Interiors

Gesamtkunstwerk: The Total Work of Art

The movement championed the concept of a unified interior environment where furniture, wallpaper, stained glass, and textiles harmonized completely. Every object was designed with equal care, dissolving the hierarchy between "high" art and "minor" crafts.

Key Disciplines

Wallpaper & Textiles: Intricate botanical repeats defined domestic spaces.
Furniture: Honest construction with visible joinery replaced Victorian clutter.
Stained Glass: Revived medieval techniques for secular and domestic use.

"Have nothing in your houses that you do not know to be useful, or believe to be beautiful." — William Morris
