07. Rococo Era

Refined Masculinity:
The Habit à la Française

The Three-Piece Suit Emerges

The foundation of modern menswear solidified in the Rococo period with the habit à la française: a coat (justaucorps), waistcoat (gilet), and breeches (culottes). While slimmer than Baroque cuts, the emphasis remained on exquisite surface decoration.

Embroidery as Status

Men were as decorative as women, wearing silks embroidered with floral motifs, metallic threads, and sequins. The waistcoat became the focal point of artistic expression, often contrasting boldly with the outer coat in color and texture.

"Male elegance was defined by the quality of embroidery and the whiteness of linen stockings—a display of leisure and refinement."
