04. Roman Era

Women's Garments

Stola, Palla & Imperial Elegance

50

01

The Stola

The traditional garment of Roman matrons (married women), distinct from the Greek chiton. A long, sleeveless tunic suspended from straps, symbolizing modesty and status.

02

The Palla

A large rectangular shawl draped over the stola. Worn outdoors as a veil or wrap, offering warmth and further signaling respectability and adherence to Roman social codes.

03

Coiffure & Complexity

Elaborate hairstyles (Flavian curls, braided buns) became key indicators of wealth, requiring enslaved hairdressers (ornatrices) and showcasing imperial trends.

04

Adornment & Jewelry

Unlike men restricted to rings, women wore opulent necklaces, earrings, and bracelets of gold, pearls, and emeralds, displaying family wealth and cosmopolitan trade connections.
