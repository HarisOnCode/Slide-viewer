Pictographs & Proto-Writing

Long before alphabets, graphic communication began with pictographs (literal representations) and ideographs (abstract concepts). These marks served not just as art, but as functional records of hunting grounds, lunar cycles, and tribal territory.

Narrative & Teaching

Cave panels were often arranged sequentially, functioning as early "storyboards" to teach hunting strategies or initiate youth into tribal lore. The rhythmic repetition of dots and lines suggests an early understanding of pattern as information.

"These recurring symbols represent the first step toward graphic design—standardizing visual language to transmit knowledge across time."
