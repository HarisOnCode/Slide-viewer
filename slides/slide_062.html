The Birth of Fit

The 14th century revolutionized silhouette. Simple, draped tunics gave way to the cotehardie—a garment cut in multiple pieces to contour the body. This shift introduced set-in sleeves, curved seams, and the concept of "fashion" as rapid stylistic change.

Layering & Lacing

Garments became complex systems: linen chemise (base), kirtle/cote (support), and surcoat (display). Lacing and "points" (ties) allowed tight fits that reshaped the torso, while dozens of buttons replaced pins for closure.

"Buttoned clothes allowed fabric to follow the body's lines for the first time, creating the modern concept of fit."
