48 Roman / Grooming
Cosmetics & Status

Cultus: The Art of Adornment

For elite Roman women (matronae), elaborate hairstyles were architectural feats requiring slaves (ornatrices) to curl and pile hair into towering diadems. Cosmetics involved white lead foundations, kohl eyes, and rouge, while men maintained clean-shaven faces as a sign of civility until Hadrian popularized the beard.
