Engineering an Empire

Roman design was defined by monumental scale and utility. A vast network of roads (viae), aqueducts, and public baths unified the empire, demonstrating power through unprecedented engineering feats and urban planning.

Visual Rhetoric & Triumphs

Art served the state. Triumphal arches, columns, and coinage acted as mass media, broadcasting victories and imperial divinity. This was design as political communication—clear, imposing, and reproducible.

"All roads lead to Rome—design was the physical manifestation of imperial connectivity and dominance."
