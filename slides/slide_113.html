The Undulating Seam

Garment construction mirrored nature, abandoning rigid straight lines for seams that spiraled and curved around the body. This technique created fluid silhouettes that echoed the famous "whiplash curve" of graphic art, emphasizing the S-bend posture.

Botanical Appliqué

Surface decoration became structural. Couture houses used heavy appliqué of velvet on silk, beadwork, and embroidery depicting climbing vines, orchids, and dragonflies to create texture and depth, merging the fabric with its ornament.

"The dress became a canvas for living art, where structure and ornament were indivisible."
