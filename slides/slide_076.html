06. Baroque Era

Women's Garments

Structured Bodices, Opulent Silks & Venetian Lace

76

01

Court Portraiture

Early Baroque fashion featured stiff, elongated bodices and rich brocades, reflecting the rigid etiquette of European courts.

02

The Mantua & Stomacher

The open-fronted gown (mantua) revealed a decorative stomacher and petticoat, often heavily embroidered with silver or gold thread.

03

Spanish Farthingale

Spanish influence maintained the wide, structured farthingale skirt shape, creating a imposing, architectural silhouette for royalty.

04

Lace & Accessories

Exquisite Venetian lace collars and cuffs were status symbols of immense value, softening the severe lines of heavy velvet gowns.
