The Craftsman's Workshop (c. 1890)
A return to traditional guild structures emphasizing the dignity of labor and hand-production over industrial mechanization.
08. Arts & Crafts Movement

Background &
Context

Honest Materials

Rejecting the deceptive veneers of mass production, the movement championed "truth to materials"—allowing wood, metal, and stone to show their natural grain, texture, and construction methods openly.

Medievalism & Social Reform

Inspired by John Ruskin and William Morris, designers looked back to medieval guilds as a model for ethical labor. They sought to reconnect the designer with the maker, believing that beautiful environments could improve moral character.

"Have nothing in your house that you do not know to be useful, or believe to be beautiful." — William Morris
