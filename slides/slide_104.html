William Morris

The father of the movement. Poet, designer, and socialist who championed the revival of traditional textile arts and production methods to counter industrial alienation.

John Ruskin

Art critic and philosopher. His writings on the "truth to nature" and the moral value of craftsmanship formed the intellectual backbone of the entire era.

C.R. Ashbee

Architect and silversmith who founded the Guild of Handicraft. He moved his workshops to the Cotswolds to integrate life, labor, and art in a rural setting.

Legacy of Reform

These figures established that design is not just aesthetic but ethical—influencing the Bauhaus and modern sustainable fashion movements.
