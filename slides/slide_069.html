1600 – 1650 | EARLY BAROQUE

The Counter-Reformation

The Catholic Church employs dramatic art and architecture as persuasion. Emergence of chiaroscuro lighting and dynamic movement in Rome.

1650 – 1700 | HIGH BAROQUE

Absolutist Courts & Power

Louis XIV establishes Versailles as the epicenter of style. Fashion becomes codified statecraft; global trade introduces exotic textiles.

1700 – 1750 | LATE BAROQUE

Expansion & Transition

Baroque spreads to colonies. Forms become lighter and more ornamental, paving the way for the delicate asymmetry of Rococo.

KEY INNOVATION

The Gesamtkunstwerk

The "total work of art" concept where architecture, painting, sculpture, and fashion unify to create an immersive, theatrical environment.
