The Width of Luxury (c. 1750)
The robe à la française with extreme side hoops (paniers) created a silhouette that occupied maximum physical space, symbolizing wealth and leisure.
07. Rococo Era Context

Origins &
Silhouettes

Robe à la Française

The defining gown of the era featured the "Watteau back"—loose pleats falling from the shoulders to the floor over a fitted bodice. It combined informal negligee origins with court formality, allowing for display of expensive silk brocades.

The Panier (Basket)

Understructures expanded laterally, creating a flat front and back but extreme width at the hips. This enforced a specific, stately walk and required double doors in architecture.

Key Details

  • Echelle Stomachers: Rows of graduated ribbon bows down the bodice front.
  • Engageantes: Multi-layered lace ruffles at the elbow.
  • Pastel Palette: Soft pinks, blues, and yellows mirroring interior decor.
