1890 – 1910: An International Style

"Art is the only way to run away without leaving home."

Flow

1893 – 1895

The Birth of the Curve

Victor Horta's Tassel House introduces "whiplash" curves in iron. Bing's L'Art Nouveau gallery opens in Paris, giving the movement its name.

Brussels Paris

1896 – 1900

The Glasgow Style

Charles Rennie Mackintosh and the "Four" develop a unique linear, vertical geometric variation distinct from continental curves.

Glasgow London

1897 – 1903

Secession & Jugendstil

The Vienna Secession (Klimt, Hoffman) breaks from tradition. Germany's Jugendstil blends floral motifs with mythic symbolism.

Vienna Munich

1900 – 1905

Peak & Proliferation

Paris Exposition Universelle (1900) showcases the style globally. Guimard designs the iconic Paris Métro entrances.

International New York

1910 – 1914

Transition to Geometry

The style fades as designers move toward the geometric abstraction that will become Art Deco.

End of Era
