Drape, Pin, & Veil: The Classical Form

37

01

The Doric Peplos

A heavy woolen tubular garment folded at the top (apoptygma). Fastened at the shoulders with large fibulae pins, creating a simple, columnar look.

02

The Ionic Chiton

Made from finely pleated linen. Fastened along the arms with small buttons or brooches to create faux sleeves, offering a lighter, fluid silhouette.

03

Himation & Veiling

A heavy rectangular cloak draped over the chiton. Essential for modesty in public, it was often pulled up over the head to serve as a veil.

04

The Kolpos & Zone

Excess fabric pulled over a girdle (belt) to create a pouch called the kolpos. This allowed women to adjust the length and drape of the garment.
