11 Stone Age / Body Art
Identity & Ritual

The Living Canvas

Before textile weaving, the skin itself was the primary canvas. Red ochre, mixed with fat, acted as a second skin—providing ritual protection, insect repellent, and tribal identification. This marked the earliest form of social coding and visual communication in human history.

Core Material Red Ochre (Hematite)
