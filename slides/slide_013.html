01. Stone Age

Prehistoric Adornment

Beads, Amulets & Social Signifiers

13

01

Shell Beads (Nassarius)

Among the oldest known jewelry (c. 82,000 years old). Pierced shells worn as necklaces, signaling group identity and status within tribes.

02

Carved Bone Adornments

Decorated tube beads made from hare bone (c. 12,940 years old). Demonstrates early craftsmanship and the repurposing of animal remains for aesthetic use.

03

Stone & Amber Amulets

Polished stones and fossilized resin (amber) were prized for their color and rarity, often believed to possess protective or magical properties.

04

Teeth & Claw Pendants

Drilled animal teeth (fox, deer, bear) worn as pendants. Likely represented hunting prowess, totem animals, or spiritual protection.
