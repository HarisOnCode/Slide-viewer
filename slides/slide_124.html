The Silhouette Revolution

From Flapper to Siren

The Art Deco era radically reshaped the female form. The 1920s introduced the tubular "garçonne" silhouette, dropping waists to the hip and liberating the body from corsetry for kinetic movement. By the 1930s, the aesthetic shifted toward the "siren" look—defined by Madeleine Vionnet’s bias cut, which allowed fabric to cling sensually to the body’s curves, creating a liquid, architectural elegance.
