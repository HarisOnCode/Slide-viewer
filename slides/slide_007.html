The Birth of Settlement

The transition from Paleolithic hunter-gatherers to Neolithic farmers marked a fundamental shift in human design. As communities settled, portable art evolved into permanent architecture, ceramics, and textiles.

Climate & Culture

Post-Ice Age warming allowed for agriculture, creating surplus resources. This surplus led to social hierarchies, specialized crafts, and the need for visual identifiers—early branding of clan and status.

"Art was not decoration but function—a tool for survival, fertility, and communal identity."
