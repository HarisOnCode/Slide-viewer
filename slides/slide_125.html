Surface Embellishment

Art Deco fashion abandoned Victorian fussiness for sleek surfaces that served as canvases for elaborate decoration. Sequins, bugle beads, and metallic threads were applied in geometric motifs—ziggurats, sunbursts, and chevrons—transforming dresses into wearable architecture.

Material Innovation

Designers embraced modernity through materials. Lamé fabrics introduced a liquid metal effect, while synthetic rayons (artificial silk) democratized luster. Bias cutting techniques allowed these heavy, beaded fabrics to cling sensuously to the body without restriction.

"The dress must not hang on the body but follow its lines. It must be accompanied by the wearer's movements." — Madeleine Vionnet
