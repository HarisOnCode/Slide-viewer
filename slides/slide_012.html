01. Stone Age Fashion

Origins of the
Silhouette

Hides & Fibers

Garments began as raw survival tools. Animal hides were scraped and softened for warmth, while the Neolithic revolution introduced twisted plant fibers. Draping was dictated purely by the dimensions of the loom or the animal skin.

Utility-Led Design

The invention of the eyed needle (c. 50,000 BP) was a pivotal design moment, allowing for fitted garments. Silhouettes were simple T-shapes or wrap-arounds, secured by leather ties or bone pins—the ancestors of modern fasteners.

"The first stitch was the beginning of technology—turning raw nature into engineered culture."
