The Age of Absolutism

The Baroque era (c. 1600–1750) was the ultimate theater of state power. Monarchs like Louis XIV utilized design as a tool of political persuasion, transforming palaces into stages where every detail—from architecture to dress—performed authority.

Chiaroscuro & Dynamism

Static forms were abandoned for dynamic movement. Artists like Bernini froze moments of intense emotion in stone, while architecture embraced convex and concave curves, creating a visual language of high contrast (chiaroscuro) and drama.

"Baroque is the art of the theatrum mundi—the world as a stage where light, shadow, and movement converge."
