03. Greek Era Garments

Men's Attire:
Civic & Athletic

The Chiton & Himation

Chiton: The base garment, typically shorter for men (knee-length) for active movement, pinned at shoulders. Philosophers and older men often wore it full-length.

Himation: A large woolen rectangle draped over the left shoulder and wrapped around the body. Wearing it correctly without pins required skill and signaled civic status.

Military & Travel Wear

Chlamys: A short, seamless rectangle of wool cloak worn by soldiers and travelers, pinned on the right shoulder with a fibula to leave the sword arm free.

Footwear: While often barefoot indoors, men wore leather sandals (crepida) with complex strapping or boots for travel and warfare.

"The male silhouette emphasized freedom of movement and physical readiness, balancing civic dignity with athletic ideals."
