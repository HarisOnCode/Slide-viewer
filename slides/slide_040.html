The Masters: Phidias & Polykleitos

Greek sculpture reached its zenith with Phidias, who oversaw the Parthenon's monumental program, and Polykleitos, who established the 'Canon' of mathematical proportions. They shifted art from archaic rigidity to idealized, contrapposto naturalism.

Narrative Pottery & Public Space

Design shaped civic life through acoustically perfect theaters like Epidaurus. Simultaneously, black-figure and red-figure pottery functioned as portable media, documenting mythology, daily life, and fashion details.
