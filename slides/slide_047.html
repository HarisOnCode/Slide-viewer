Verism in Portraiture

Unlike Greek idealism, Roman visual language embraced verism—a hyper-realistic style that celebrated age, experience, and authority. This is evident in the intricate mosaics and frescoes of Pompeii, which introduced sophisticated shading and perspective to capture the "truth" of the subject.

The Language of Power: Arches & Domes

Roman visual dominance extended to the skyline through the invention of concrete. They engineered the arch and the dome (e.g., The Pantheon), moving away from post-and-lintel limits to create vast, unencumbered interior spaces that symbolized the empire's reach.

"Roman design married the illusion of life in painting with the reality of eternal strength in architecture."
