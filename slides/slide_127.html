10. Art Deco (c. 1919–1939)

Forms & Interiors Speed & Spectacle

The Interior Revolution

Art Deco transformed interior spaces into total works of art. From the grand foyers of movie palaces to the intimate cabins of ocean liners, every surface was treated with exotic woods, chrome, lacquer, and geometric patterns, creating an atmosphere of modern luxury and escapism.

Streamline Moderne

Inspired by aerodynamics and speed, the later phase of Deco (Streamline Moderne) smoothed sharp corners into horizontal curves. This aesthetic jumped from vehicles to architecture and everyday objects—radios, toasters, and clocks—symbolizing progress and efficiency.

"The machine aesthetic became a style of beauty—clean lines, polished surfaces, and the promise of a frictionless future."
