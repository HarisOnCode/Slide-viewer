Timeline

1919 – 1939: The Machine Age Aesthetic

"A movement of sleek geometry, luxury materials, and unbridled optimism."

1925

1919 – 1924

Post-WWI Optimism

Emergence of a new modern style reacting against Art Nouveau's curves. Focus on machine aesthetics, speed, and geometric order.

1925

Exposition Internationale (Paris)

The pivotal "Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes" formally introduces the style to the world.

1926 – 1930s

Global Skyscraper Style

The style spreads to NYC (Chrysler Building, Empire State) and globally, symbolizing progress, luxury, and the jazz age.

1930s – 1939

Streamline Moderne

Evolution into aerodynamic curves and horizontal lines inspired by ships and trains, ending with the onset of WWII.
